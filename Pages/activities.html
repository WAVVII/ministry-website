Department of Administration And Control

1. Staff Promotion, Recommendation And Confirmation of Appointments Carried out

  • Pending 2019 promotion of staff and confirmation of appt carried out
  • The Senior Management Committee has also considered and recommended One Hundred Twenty-Six (126) Senior staff to the Commission for promotion.
  • Within the same period, the junior staff committee sat and considered 2020 promotion of 98 staff, letters have been issued to the affected staff.

3. Vehicles Provided For Emergency Units

  • The Ministry procured 8 No. Toyota Hilux and Ambulance for Emergency Operation unit.
  • The Ministry received a Toyota Land Cruiser vehicle donated to it by Medicine San Frontiers a non-Governmental Organization.

5. Information unit of the Ministry

  • The Ministry purchased a public address system for the Information unit of the Ministry.

6. Public Health's Wings

  • Renovation of the Hon. Commissioner's and part of Department of Public Health's wings of Headquarters, Makurdi.

7. SCHOOL OF HEALTH TECHNOLOGY, AGASHA.

  • There is relative peace at School of Health Technology, Agasha.

Public Health Department

1. HIV/AIDS Program

  • Number of HIV Testing Services (HTS) scale up with HCT site to 619, ART 168 and PMTCT 496 sites (2020).
  • Total number of persons placed on ART increases from 187,121 (2020) to 206,000.
  • Integration of PMTCT into the maternal Newborn Child Health Week (MNCHW) to improve coverage.

2. Reproductive Health Unit

  • The number of Health facilities offering Family Planning Services increased from 315 by 2016 to 409 in the LGAS.

3. Public Health Emergency Operation Centre (PHEOC)

  • Receipt and utilization of World Bank loan for COVID-19 response.
  • Successful control of Yellow Fever outbreak in two worse hit local Government of Okpokwu and Ogbadibo, and subsequent vaccinations of Benue at risk population.
  • Acquisition of 5 utility vehicle for Public Health response.
  • Renovation and upgrading of treatment Centre at BSUTH.

4. Neglected Tropical Disease (Onchocerchiasis/Elephantiasis/Trachoma Control)

  • Training of Community Directed Distributors (CDDs), frontline healthcare worker (FLHW) in 22 LGAs, including 120 Teachers from Public Primary Schools under SUBEB to help distribution of Mectizan drugs in the State
  • Scale up of mass Administration of Medicines (MAM) (Mectizan an Albendazole) to 5 Oncho/LF, Schistomiasis Co-endemic LGAs

5. Tuberculosis Leprosy and Buruli ulcer Control

  • Established 315 microscopy centre, 25 GeneXpertcentre and 396 DOTS centre for diagnosis and treatment respectively.
  • Training of general Health workers on DOTS and TB/HIV collaborative activities
  • Establishment of drug resistant TB (DR-TB) ward with support from IHVN at St. Vincent Hospital Allade.

6. Mental Health Program

  • The private sector engagement between Benue State Ministry of Health and Human Services and Methodist Church (MCN) on comprehensive Community Mental Health (CCMHP) is significantly reached (10,982 new cases) and 33, 992 on treatment in the State.
  • Through the partnership, the State has integrated mental health services into 41 PHCs, prison and IDP camps has increased access to Mental Health Services in the State.
  • 41 training of CHEWS at PHC level and 23 Doctors at secondary level with capacity to identify and manage common psychosocial disorder.

7. School of Health Technology, Agasha.

  • The accreditation of some courses at SHT Agasha and constitution of a Board.

DEPARTMENT OF PHARMACEUTICAL SERVICES

1. International Partnership For Provision Of Drugs

  • Provision and distribution of about Twelve Billion Naira worth of anti-retroviral drugs and Two Billon Naira worth of anti-malaria in partnership with International Partners.

2. Mosquito Nets Used For Replacement Campaign

  • Under the PMI, the State distributed 3.6 Million insecticides treated mosquito nets valued at over 4.5 billion. The campaign was completed in December 2022. These nets were used for replacement campaign and we will be embarking on continuous distribution of channel to reach out to those who lost out during mass campaign.

3. Engagement With Private Institutions

  • Commence engagement with private organizations for the setting up of drugs distribution Centre for the State in line with national directives.

4. Establishment of Drugs and Health Commodity Bill

  • A bill for the establishment of drugs and Health Commodities Agency 2021 is before the State EXCO for presentation to the State House of Assembly.

Directorate Of Medical Laboratory Services

1. Inspection of Health facilities

  • Inspection and registration of over 30 unregistered private health facilities.

2. Measures For IGR

  • The management has put in place measures to improve Interally Generated Revenue (IGR) of the ministry through enumeration agents.

DEPARTMENT OF PLANNING RESEARCH AND STATISTICS

1. Renovation Of Structures And Provision Of ICT Equipment Renovation

  • Renovation Of Structures And Provision Of ICT Equipment Renovation of structures, infrastructures and supply of office furniture and ICT equipment at School of Nursing and Midwifery. Work on various structures at school of Nursing and Midwifery have completed and handed over to SMOH, some have reached various levels of completion ranging from 90% to 100% while others like construction of a block of hostel that can accommodate 100 students and a block of storey building containing classrooms, offices and conference hall, Construction of Administrative block.

2. Awarding of Contracts

  • Government has also awarded contract for expansion of General Hospital Naka in the area of construction of one block of children's ward.

3. Fencing of General Hospitals

  • In order to forestall encroachment of our health institutions, Government has awarded contracts for construction of perimeter fence and gate houses at General Hospitals Okpoga and Gbajimba.

4. Provision of Water

  • Contracts for construction of boreholes and water reticulation at school of Health Technology Agasha has been completed and handed over to the school; while that of construction of 3Nos boreholes and water reticulation is on-going at school of Nursing and Midwifery.

5. Renovation of Permanent Site for Primary Health Care Board Makurdi

  • The State Government through the Ministry has been fully renovated an office complex and handed over to the State Primary Health Care Board for use. This has created a better working environment for the Board as well as reduced the burden of yearly rentage.

6. Renovation of General Hospital Gbajimba:

  • The General Hospital Gbajimba which was dilapidated and infested with bats has been renovated and handed over to the State Hospitals Management Board. This has improved the working environment as well as service provision at the hospital thereby contributing to quality of service offered at the hospital.
