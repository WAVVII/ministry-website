MINISTRY OF HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES

About Us

A). ESTABLISHMENT:
Benue State is located in the Middle Belt (North – Central) part of Nigeria. It was created on February, 3 rd 1976, from the then Benue – Plateau. According to the 2006 census, the total population of Benue State is estimated to be 4,780,389. The ethnic groups in the State are, Tiv, Idoma, Igede, Etulo, Abakpa, Jukun, Hausa, Akweya and Nyifon, with Tiv being the dominant tribe, occupying 14 local governments, while the Idomas and Igedes occupy the remaining 9 local governments. In terms of land mass, Benue sits a 32,518sqkm. From when the State was created to date, the state has had 15 past Governors, and Governor Samuel Ortom is the 16 th Governor of the State. The Benue State Ministry of Health and Human Services was one of the pioneer Ministries created in the State for its administration and is as old as the creation of the State in 1976. After the establishment and in accordance with the national health policy, the quest and need for establishment of some parastetals under the Ministry to help in deliverance of quality health care to the citizens of the State was imperative. The first parastatal established was Hospitals Management Board (BSHMB) in the year, 1988 followed by Benue State Aids Control Agency (BENSACA) 2001, Benue State University Teaching Hospital (BSUTH) 2008, Benue State Primary Health Care Board (BSPHCB) 2013, and Benue State Health Insurance Agency (BENSHIA) 2018.

B). VISION STATEMENT:
The vision of the Benue State Ministry of Health and Human Services is to develop quality and affordable health care services and preventive measures of international standard, creation of favorable conditions for the population’s well-being with the active participation of the individual and the community and make a strategic contribution to the attainment of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

C). MISSION STATEMENT:
To develop and implement appropriate policies and programs as well as undertake other necessary actions that will strengthen the National Health System to be able to deliver effective, quality and affordable health services.

D) Core Values:
The core values intended to drive the vision of the State Ministry of health are:
*Availability of access to quality, equitable and affordable health care.
*Protection from financial hardship in seeking health care.
*Protection from financial hardship in seeking health care.
*Gender equity and responsiveness, cultural sensitivity, professionalism and ethical considerations must be taken into account by all actors in the health system.

E). FUNDING:
The Ministry of Health and Human Services get funding from Annual State Budgets, Grants and Donors through funding of programs.

F). STRUCTURE:
The Ministry of Health and Human Services is headed by the Honourable Commissioner as the Chief Executive Officer. The second in command is the Permanent Secretary, who is the Chief Administrative Secretary and Accounting Officer of the Ministry. The Ministry has Eight (8) Directorates namely;
*Directorate of Administration and Supply
*Finance and Accounts
*Planning Research and Statistics
*Pharmaceutical Services
*Medical Services
*Public Health
*Nursing Services
*Medical Laboratory Services
